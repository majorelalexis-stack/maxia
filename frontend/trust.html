Dispute Resolution

AI-powered via Groq LLM. Fair, fast, transparent.

1. Dispute Filed
Buyer opens a dispute within the escrow window. Both parties are notified. Escrow funds remain locked.
2. Evidence Collection
Both buyer and seller submit evidence: API logs, delivery proofs, error screenshots. Deadline: 24 hours.
3. AI Evaluation
Groq LLM (Llama 3.3 70B) analyzes evidence from both sides. Considers: delivery proof, SLA compliance, response quality, timestamps.
4. Resolution
AI decides one of 3 outcomes. Decision is final and executed on-chain automatically.
✅
Release to Seller
Service was delivered correctly. Full payment released to seller minus MAXIA commission.
🔄
Refund Buyer
Service was not delivered or failed quality check. Full refund to buyer.
⚖
50/50 Split
Partial delivery or ambiguous fault. Funds split equally between buyer and seller.
Proof of Delivery
Every service execution is tracked with liveness monitoring. The system records: request timestamp, response timestamp, HTTP status codes, output hash, and latency. This data is used as evidence in disputes.

USDC & Regulatory Risks

Transparency about USDC settlement and potential regulatory actions.

MAXIA settles all trades in USDC (Circle). USDC is a regulated stablecoin. In compliance with US and international regulations, Circle may freeze USDC on specific addresses if required by law enforcement (OFAC sanctions). If this affects an escrow PDA, the funds would be temporarily frozen until the regulatory matter is resolved. MAXIA's emergency_close mechanism attempts to recover funds, and our team will actively work with Circle to resolve any freezes. This risk is inherent to all USDC-based protocols and is not specific to MAXIA.
